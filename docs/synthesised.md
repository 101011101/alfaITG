## Thesis

Craft *is* the argument. A committee fears a vendor who under-delivers; a
frame-perfect pitch reads as frame-perfect engineering. The site sells one thing —
**a call or email** — and earns it by how it's built, not what it claims.

**KPI:** visitor → qualified lead **1.5–2.5%**; proof before the ask; one CTA.

---

## Meat & Potatoes

In scroll order. Each: what → why.

1. **Hero** — F-22 video, thesis on-screen → 5-second "this is real."
2. **Robot** — reactive 3D robot ringed by the company story → thesis + navigator.
3. **Products** — tilted 3D marquee → survey the offerings fast.
4. **Proof** — one hard number + news → "it works," placed before the ask.
5. **Contact** — cross-fade finale → the conversion: call/email.
6. **Footer** — form, hours, legal → the structured fallback.

---

## Macro (persistent across every frame)

- **Notch scroll** — every section is a beat: ease in to savour, flick to blitz,
  never trapped; native scroll under reduced-motion.
- **Header** — hides on scroll-down, returns on scroll-up — the hesitation moment.
- **Persistent Contact** — corner button, always on after the hero. One ask, many
  doors.
- **Side bar** — length cue, skip arrows, teleport.
- **Logo banner** — fixed social proof.

*All five exist for one reason: hold the cinematic frame while keeping the one ask
always reachable.*
